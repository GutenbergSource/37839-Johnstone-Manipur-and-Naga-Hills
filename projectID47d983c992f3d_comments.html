A straightforward report of an English officers doings in Eastern India on the border with Burma in what was then known as The Naga Hills and Manipur.

The Naga hills where the home of various Naga people, such as the Angami, Thankgul, Ao, Sema, etc. who are culturally related, but speak quite different languages.

Manipur was a old Hindu kingdom south of the Naga hills, with its own peculiar history, and relations with various tribal people living in the area, such as the Kukis. Today, the area described is roughly covered by the Indian states of Nagaland and Manipur.
